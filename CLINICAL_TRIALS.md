# Clinical Trials: Affordable Prosthetic Solutions

This document tracks clinical trials focused on affordable and cost-effective prosthetic solutions for resource-limited settings.

---

## Trial 1: ReMotion Prosthetic Knee (NCT04700085)

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Title** | Reverse Innovation in Western Health Care: the ReMotion Prosthetic Knee |
| **Sponsor** | Sint Maartenskliniek |
| **Status** | COMPLETED |
| **Period** | Feb 2019 – Aug 2019 |
| **Enrollment** | 9 participants |
| **Location** | Sint Maartenskliniek, Netherlands |

### Summary
The **ReMotion Knee** is an $80 mechanical prosthetic knee designed by D-Rev (San Francisco, CA) for use in low-income countries. It features a polycentric design with a 3–5 year lifespan but lacks pneumatic/hydraulic systems. This study evaluated it as a "reverse innovation" — testing whether a low-cost knee originally designed for low-resource settings could serve as an affordable alternative in high-income countries.

**Key Findings:**
- Consumer prices for standard non-microprocessor knees (NMPK) still reach up to $5,000 in Europe/US
- ReMotion Knee: **$80** vs. standard mechanical knees: **$1,000–$4,000**
- Approved per ISO 10328 standards with CE Mark
- Evaluated on functional mobility (L-test, mEFAP), balance (Berg Balance Scale), walking comfort, and performance
- Expected slight performance deficit vs. current prostheses, but at dramatically lower cost

### Relevance to Outreach
✅ Ultra-low-cost option ($80) for outreach programs
✅ Reverse innovation model applicable to multiple regions
✅ Suitable for basic mobility needs in underserved communities

## Trial 2: Low Cost Socket for Lower Limb Amputees (NCT04725461)

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Title** | Low Cost Prosthetic Socket for Lower Limb Amputees |
| **Sponsor** | Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (Chicago, IL) |
| **Status** | COMPLETED |
| **Period** | Jul 2018 – Jan 2025 |
| **Enrollment** | 8 participants |
| **Principal Investigator** | Laura Miller |
| **Location** | 355 E Erie Street, Chicago, IL 60611 |

### Summary
This trial developed a simplified socket fabrication method using expandable rigid foam. The method allows casting in **<30 minutes** and socket finishing in **90 minutes**, making it practical for busy clinical settings and areas where patients cannot return for multiple fittings.

**Key Findings:**
- Conventional socket fabrication requires multiple fittings over days/weeks
- New method: limb cast in <30 min, socket complete in 90 min
- Uses pre-made 3D printed outer shell + polyurethane foam (Foam IT 8)
- ICRC transtibial polypropylene component system for suspension
- Provisional US patent filed

### Relevance to Outreach
✅ **Extended to developing countries** — specifically designed for amputees with limited access
✅ 90-minute fitting time enables single-visit clinics
✅ 3D-printed components reduce manufacturing cost
✅ **Chicago-based** — directly relevant to our Chicago team at SRAL

## Trial 3: 3D-Printed Prosthetic Care (NCT03517774)

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Title** | Improving Prosthetic Care for Patients With a Lower Limb Amputation |
| **Sponsor** | Sunnybrook Health Sciences Centre |
| **Status** | COMPLETED |
| **Period** | May 2018 – Mar 2021 |
| **Enrollment** | 12 participants |
| **Location** | Sunnybrook St. John's Rehab, Toronto, Canada |

### Summary
This trial evaluated a full digital manufacturing tool-chain (3DPrintAbility by NIA Technologies) for transtibial prosthetics. The system enables 3D scanning → design → printing in under 1.5 days, compared to the conventional 1–2 week process. Previous field testing was done in Cambodia, Tanzania, and Uganda.

**Key Findings:**
- 3D scanning eliminates plaster casting — eliminates time-consuming manual processes
- Conventional fitting: 5 days → New process: **<1.5 days**
- 3D printed sockets performed equivalently to traditional thermoplastic sockets
- Significant cost and time savings potential for healthcare systems
- Previously tested in developing world settings (pediatric transtibial amputees)

### Relevance to Outreach
✅ Digital manufacturing reduces cost and turnaround time dramatically
✅ Proven in developing world settings
✅ Enables faster rehabilitation — less time = less functional decline
✅ Applicable to outreach clinics with limited prosthetist access
